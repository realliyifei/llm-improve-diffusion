> Why "Mice are scaring a lion" is counterintuitive? Explain.

"Mice are scaring a lion" is counterintuitive because it goes against the natural order of the food chain. Mice are a food source for lions, not something that would normally scare them. This phrase implies an unusual situation in which the mice are able to dominate the lion, which is not usually the case.
