> Why "A tree with leaves made of feathers" is counterintuitive? Explain.

"A tree with leaves made of feathers" is counterintuitive because it goes against our innate understanding of how trees work. Trees typically have leaves that are stiff and composed of cells containing chlorophyll, which enables them to absorb sunlight and produce food. Feathers, on the other hand, are light, soft, and composed of a protein called keratin. They are not typically associated with the process of photosynthesis that is essential to the life of a tree.
