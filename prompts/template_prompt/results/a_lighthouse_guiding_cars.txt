> Why "A lighthouse guiding cars" is counterintuitive? Explain.

The phrase “a lighthouse guiding cars” is counterintuitive because lighthouses have traditionally been used to guide ships, not cars. Lighthouses are typically stationary objects that emit a light beacon to warn ships of dangerous rocks and reefs near shore. Cars, on the other hand, are mobile objects that rely on roads and signs to guide them. The idea of a lighthouse guiding cars is counterintuitive because it is an unfamiliar concept that goes against the traditional use of a lighthouse.
